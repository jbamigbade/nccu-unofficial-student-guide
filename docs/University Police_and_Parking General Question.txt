Student Parking Decals:
A valid NCCU parking decal must always be properly displayed in all vehicles when parked on University property. Failure to display a decal or misuse of a decal may result in fines, booting, towing, and/or loss of campus parking privileges.

Decals can be picked up from the Parking Office at Eagle Landing Residence Hall with a receipt.

Please note: Students who reside on campus must park in the designated parking areas for residential parking students.

Parking decals are the property of NCCU Parking Services. Decals may not be sold, given away, transferred, or borrowed. The decal holder on record is responsible for all violations. If you choose to purchase a parking decal, it is your responsibility to familiarize yourself with campus parking regulations and NC state laws for motorized vehicle operators.

A decal is required to park on campus. Decals are valid for up to one year and expire on June 30 of the specified decal year.

Metered Parking
Metered Parking at NCCU via ParkMobile

NCCU offers contactless metered parking through ParkMobile in designated zones — all managed via the ParkMobile app or website.

ParkMobile Zones on Campus

ParkMobile applies to two main areas:

Nelson Street, fronting the Miller-Morgan Building
Church parking lot at Formosa Avenue and Fayetteville Street
Signs and meter stickers display zone numbers — each in the 5000–5003 range. You must enter the correct zone number to start a valid session which is displayed on signage near the designated area.

Getting Started

Download the ParkMobile app (iOS and Android) or visit the website.
Register an account — there is no cost to sign up.
Add your vehicle (up to five plates supported).
Locate your zone number on onsite signs before parking.
Pay online for your desired duration.
Extensions are allowed via the app, subject to the maximum meter time. Unfortunately, sessions cannot be paused or canceled once started. View the Single Space Parking Meter Instructions.
Payments and Fees

Parking fee: Standard hourly rate (as posted on signs/meters)
Accepted payment methods: Credit/debit cards, PayPal, American Express, Discover, and ParkMobile wallet.​​​​​​
Metered Parking at Latham Parking Deck

Metered parking is also available in the Latham Parking Deck, located near the heart of campus. This location provides convenient short-term parking for campus visitors, students, and event attendees.

Important Details

A kiosk payment option is available at the entrance for those who prefer to pay on-site.
Parking rates may vary based on scheduled campus events, such as athletic games, concerts, or university ceremonies.
Real-time parking options and pricing are visible via the entry kiosk, allowing drivers to make informed decisions before initiating a session.
Enforcement
Campus parking officers verify payments by checking your license plate plus the zone number through their handheld devices — no printed receipts are needed.

Please follow posted signage and pay attention to rate details to avoid any citation due to expired time or payment issues.

Ticketing
Info Accordions

 I got a ticket. Where do I go to pay for it?
All payments must be made at the Cashier’s Office, Room 12, Shepard Administration Building, during business hours (Monday–Friday).

Can I appeal a ticket?
Yes, you may petition the ticket. Contact the parking office at 919-530-5023 or parking@nccu.edu for further assistance.

When will a vehicle be towed at the owner’s expense?
A vehicle may be towed if it:

Is parked on campus without a valid permit
Is parked in a reserved space without the correct permit
Is illegally parked in an emergency space or blocking emergency access
Has received three or more violations and has been booted for over 48 hours
Is illegally parked for over 10 days or classified as "derelict" per N.C. General Statute 20-137.7

My car got towed. What do I do now?
If your vehicle has been towed, please call 919-530-6106 for further assistance

Vehicle Immobilization (Booting)
Info Accordions

When can a vehicle be booted?
A vehicle may be immobilized if:

A check is returned for a traffic-related payment
It displays a lost, stolen, or revoked permit
It has three or more unpaid citations
A permit payment is deemed unsatisfactory

How is the boot removed?
The boot may only be removed by the NCCU Police Department after:

Payment of a $25 boot removal fee
Payment of all outstanding fines and late fees

What if my vehicle is booted for over 72 hours?
Vehicles immobilized for over 72 hours will be towed or relocated to a storage facility.

How do I pay for boot removal and fines?
All payments must be made at the Cashier’s Office, Room 12, Shepard Administration Building, during business hours (Monday–Friday).

Can I appeal an immobilization?
Yes, you may petition the immobilization; however, you must still pay the boot removal fee.
