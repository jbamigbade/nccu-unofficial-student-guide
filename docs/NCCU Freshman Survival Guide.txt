NCCU Freshman Survival Guide

Academic Advice

Student Comment 1:
Register for classes as soon as your registration window opens. Popular classes and professors can fill up quickly.

Student Comment 2:
Check your email every day. Important announcements about classes, financial aid, and campus events are often sent through email.

Student Comment 3:
Don't wait until you're struggling before visiting office hours. Professors are usually more willing to help students who come early.

Student Comment 4:
Keep a calendar for assignment due dates. Many freshmen underestimate how quickly deadlines add up.

Student Comment 5:
Use the tutoring center even if you're passing the class. Many students use tutoring to stay ahead rather than catch up.

Campus Life Advice

Student Comment 6:
Attend orientation events and Eagle Mania activities. Many students make their first friends through these events.

Student Comment 7:
Join at least one student organization during your first semester. It helps you meet people outside your classes.

Student Comment 8:
Don't spend all your time in your residence hall. Students who get involved on campus often report a better college experience.

Student Comment 9:
Get to know your academic advisor early. Advisors can help prevent registration mistakes later.

Student Comment 10:
Introduce yourself to classmates. Many study groups start from simple conversations before class.

Housing Advice

Student Comment 11:
If you're living on campus, bring shower shoes and a small fan. These are items many students wish they had packed.

Student Comment 12:
Apply for housing as early as possible because preferred locations may fill quickly.

Student Comment 13:
Talk with roommates about expectations during the first week instead of waiting for problems to develop.

Time Management Advice

Student Comment 14:
Many freshmen overestimate how much free time they have. Schedule study time before planning social activities.

Student Comment 15:
Avoid skipping class, even when attendance is not required. Missing one lecture can make it difficult to catch up.

Student Comment 16:
Break large assignments into smaller tasks. Waiting until the night before usually creates unnecessary stress.

Money-Saving Advice

Student Comment 17:
Check for student discounts before buying software, technology, or event tickets.

Student Comment 18:
Buy textbooks only after attending the first class meeting. Some professors rarely use the required book.

Student Comment 19:
Compare prices between the campus bookstore and online retailers before making purchases.

Student Comment 20:
Take advantage of free campus events and resources. Many activities include free food, networking, or professional development opportunities.


Upperclassmen recommend registering for classes as soon as registration opens because popular courses fill quickly.

Students recommend visiting professors during office hours even when they are not struggling.

Many students advise joining at least one student organization during the first semester to build connections.

Students recommend checking Moodle and email daily.
