NCCU Student Housing Experiences and Advice

Housing Application Process

Student Comment 1:
Many students recommend applying for housing as early as possible because preferred residence halls can fill quickly.

Student Comment 2:
Upperclassmen advise freshmen to carefully review housing deadlines and requirements before submitting applications.

Student Comment 3:
Several students reported that completing housing forms early reduced stress and increased available room choices.

Residence Hall Life

Student Comment 4:
Many students enjoy living on campus during their first year because it makes it easier to meet friends and participate in campus activities.

Student Comment 5:
Students say that living on campus helps them become familiar with university resources and services more quickly.

Student Comment 6:
Several students reported that residence hall events helped them build friendships during their first semester.

Roommate Advice

Student Comment 7:
Students recommend discussing expectations about cleanliness, visitors, and quiet hours with roommates during the first week.

Student Comment 8:
Many students say that communication is the most important factor in maintaining a positive roommate relationship.

Student Comment 9:
Students encourage roommates to address small concerns early before they become larger conflicts.

Study Environment

Student Comment 10:
Students often use residence hall study rooms when they need a quieter environment than their own room.

Student Comment 11:
Many students recommend finding several study locations on campus because noise levels can vary throughout the semester.

Student Comment 12:
Some students prefer studying in the library during exam periods because residence halls can become more active.

Move-In Advice

Student Comment 13:
Upperclassmen recommend bringing only essential items during move-in and purchasing additional items later if needed.

Student Comment 14:
Students frequently suggest bringing storage containers to help maximize limited space.

Student Comment 15:
Many students recommend labeling personal belongings to avoid confusion in shared living spaces.

Campus Living Tips

Student Comment 16:
Students advise freshmen to keep important documents in a secure location.

Student Comment 17:
Many students recommend getting familiar with laundry facilities early in the semester.

Student Comment 18:
Students often encourage new residents to attend floor meetings because important housing information is shared there.

Student Comment 19:
Several students suggest introducing yourself to neighbors because they often become valuable friends and resources throughout the year.

Student Comment 20:
Students report that participating in residence hall activities can help reduce homesickness during the first semester.

Safety Advice

Student Comment 21:
Students recommend always carrying identification and room access cards.

Student Comment 22:
Many students encourage residents to lock their rooms whenever they leave, even for short periods.

Student Comment 23:
Students advise reporting maintenance issues promptly instead of waiting for them to become larger problems.

Off-Campus Housing Advice

Student Comment 24:
Students considering off-campus housing recommend touring apartments in person before signing a lease.

Student Comment 25:
Many students suggest comparing transportation options, utilities, and total costs before choosing an apartment.

Student Comment 26:
Students often recommend speaking with current residents to learn about their experiences before committing to a housing option.

Student Comment 27:
Several students advise reviewing lease agreements carefully and asking questions about fees and policies.

Success Tips

Student Comment 28:
Students report that maintaining a balance between studying, social activities, and personal wellness contributes to a better housing experience.

Student Comment 29:
Many students encourage freshmen to take advantage of residence hall staff and campus resources when they need help.

Student Comment 30:
Upperclassmen frequently remind new students that building a supportive community is one of the most valuable parts of campus living.
