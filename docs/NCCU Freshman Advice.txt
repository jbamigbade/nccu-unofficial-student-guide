NCCU Freshman Advice

Student Comment 1:
Register for classes as soon as your registration window opens because popular sections fill quickly.

Student Comment 2:
Check your NCCU email every day. Most important announcements come through email.

Student Comment 3:
Visit your professors during office hours early in the semester instead of waiting until you are struggling.

Student Comment 4:
Join at least one student organization during your first semester to meet people outside your classes.

Student Comment 5:
Use a planner or digital calendar to keep track of assignments and exams.

Student Comment 6:
Introduce yourself to classmates during the first week because many study groups form early.

Student Comment 7:
Attend freshman orientation events even if they seem optional.

Student Comment 8:
Learn where key offices are located, including Financial Aid, the Registrar, and Academic Advising.

Student Comment 9:
Do not wait until finals week to seek tutoring help.

Student Comment 10:
Many freshmen underestimate how much studying college courses require.

Student Comment 11:
Always save copies of important forms and emails.

Student Comment 12:
Get familiar with Banner and Blackboard during your first week.

Student Comment 13:
Talk with upperclassmen about course recommendations.

Student Comment 14:
Use campus resources early rather than after problems become serious.

Student Comment 15:
Balance academics, work, and social activities carefully.
